Chatham House Rules

Overview

The Chatham House Rule is a core principle that governs the conduct of discussions at the Oxford Semiconductor Conference. It is designed to encourage open and honest dialogue while protecting the privacy and confidentiality of participants.

Core Principle

"When a meeting, or part thereof, is held under the Chatham House Rule, participants are free to use the information received, but neither the identity nor the affiliation of the speaker(s), nor that of any other participant, may be revealed."

What This Means

  • You may freely share the information and insights discussed during the conference
  • You may not attribute specific comments or statements to any individual
  • You may not reveal who attended the conference or their organizational affiliations
  • This applies to all forms of communication: social media, publications, conversations, etc.

Benefits

  • Encourages candid discussion of sensitive topics
  • Allows participants to speak freely without fear of attribution
  • Facilitates open exchange of ideas and experiences
  • Protects commercial and personal interests

Examples of Permitted Sharing

  • "The conference discussed emerging trends in semiconductor manufacturing"
  • "Participants shared insights about supply chain challenges"
  • "The discussion covered regulatory developments in the industry"

Examples of Prohibited Sharing

  • "John from Company X said that..."
  • "A representative from Intel mentioned..."
  • "The CEO of ABC Corp attended and discussed..."

Compliance

All participants are expected to respect and adhere to the Chatham House Rule throughout the conference and in any subsequent communications about the event. Violation of this rule may result in exclusion from future conferences.

Questions

If you have any questions about the Chatham House Rule or how it applies to specific situations, please contact the conference organizers at admin@oxfordsemi.org.

Code of Conduct

Our Commitment

The Oxford Semiconductor Conference is committed to providing a professional, respectful, and inclusive environment for all participants. We believe that diversity of thought, experience, and background enhances the quality of our discussions and the value of our conference.

Expected Behavior

All participants are expected to:

  • Treat all individuals with respect and dignity
  • Engage in constructive and professional dialogue
  • Listen actively and consider diverse perspectives
  • Maintain confidentiality as required by the Chatham House Rule
  • Respect the venue and facilities
  • Follow all applicable laws and regulations

Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are considered unacceptable and will not be tolerated:

  • Harassment, discrimination, or bullying of any kind
  • Inappropriate or offensive comments, gestures, or materials
  • Intimidation or threats directed at other participants
  • Disruption of presentations or discussions
  • Unauthorized recording or photography
  • Violation of the Chatham House Rule
  • Any behavior that creates an unsafe or unwelcoming environment

Reporting Violations

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, please report it immediately to conference organizers. Reports can be made:

  • In person to any conference organizer
  • Via email to admin@oxfordsemi.org
  • Through the designated reporting channels at the venue

Consequences

Violations of this Code of Conduct may result in:

  • Immediate removal from the conference
  • Exclusion from future conferences
  • Notification to relevant authorities if required by law
  • Other appropriate actions as determined by conference organizers

Confidentiality

All reports of Code of Conduct violations will be handled confidentially to the extent possible while ensuring appropriate action is taken. The privacy of all parties involved will be respected.

Commitment to Improvement

We are committed to continuously improving our conference environment. Feedback on this Code of Conduct and suggestions for improvement are welcome and can be sent to admin@oxfordsemi.org.

Contact Information

For questions about this Code of Conduct or to report violations:
